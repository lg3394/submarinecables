Tracking 15 Years of Submarine Cable Expansion

Ninety-nine percent of global data flows beneath the ocean – here’s how those subsea connections are growing.

Submarine cables are the invisible backbone of the internet, carrying vast amounts of global data traffic across continents. In recent years, especially with the rise of AI-powered technologies, the demand for data collection has fueled the expansion of submarine cable infrastructure.

Most of these undersea cables are owned by individual private companies or consortiums of them, including major tech firms like Google, Facebook (Meta), Amazon, and telecommunications giants. Governments also play a role, often in partnership with private firms, which raises questions about data governance, digital sovereignty, control, and cybersecurity threats.

The data for this analysis comes from a hidden API that powers the Submarine Cable Map, revealing the steady increase in cables over the years. I plan to continue analysing and visualising the growth and location of submarina cables to reveal potential insights into geopolitical influence, national security concerns, and the shifting landscape of digital power.
