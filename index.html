Mapping 15 Years of Submarine Cable Expansion

Tracking the growth of global connectivity, one cable at a time.

Submarine cables are the invisible backbone of the internet, carrying vast amounts of global data traffic across continents. As the world becomes more interconnected, the demand for high-speed communication has fueled rapid expansion in submarine cable infrastructure.

Most of these undersea cables are owned by private companies, including major tech firms like Google, Facebook (Meta), Amazon, and telecommunications giants. Governments also play a role, often in partnership with private firms, raising questions about digital sovereignty and control.

The data for this analysis comes from a **hidden API** that powers the Submarine Cable Map, revealing the steady increase in cables over the years. Beyond raw numbers, mapping these cables' landing points and ownership structures can offer crucial insights into geopolitical influence, national security concerns, and the shifting landscape of digital power.
